Welcome

Planning a trip to the beautiful country of Brazil? This online guide is packed with lots of useful Brazilian Portuguese phrases to help you get the most out of your trip.

Whether you're planning on spending your days in a deck chair on the beach, hiking up Dois Irmãos or attending conference calls, you're going to need some basic Portuguese to get by.

If your trip to brazil inspires you to learn portuguese, one of my favourite language learning websites is Duolingo. This is a free website and application that uses various techniques to get poeple speaking new languages fluently.

It shouldnt take long to work through the content, just keep scrolling down until you hit the bottom!

My Must See Locations in Rio:

Buzios: Armação dos Búzios (or Búzios), is on the oceanic peninsula east of Rio de Janeiro. It is a popular holiday destination due to its numerous beaches that are great for snorkelling and boat trips.

Image source:Mariordo Mario Roberto Duran Ortiz, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Paraty: Paraty is a small mountainous town between Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo. It has dozens of beautiful beaches with crystal waters and white sands and many waterfalls.

Image source: Vani Ribeiro, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Isla Grande: Isla Grande is an island off the coast of Rio de Janeiro surrounded by beaches, covered in forest and hikinhg trails. Lagoa Azul, in the north of the island, has protected waters great for snorkelling.

Image source:Mariordo Mario Roberto Duran Ortiz, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Conversation

Common phrases

Hopefully if you manange to remember a few of these, you should be able to make it through your first few days in Brazil.

Pronunciation
Hello Olá (oh-la)
Goodbye Tchau (ch-ow)
Good morning Bom dia (bom jia)
Good afternoon Boa tarde (bow-a tar-jie)
Good evening Boa noite (bow-a noy-chee)
Please Por favor (paw favaw)
Thank you Obrigada - for women

Obrigado - for men

 (ob-ri-gah-da

ob-ri-gah-do)
You're welcome De nada (je nada)
How much is this? Quanto é este? (kwon-toe eh es-teh)
Do you accept credit card? Aceita cartão de crédito? (ass-ay-ta cart-ow de cred-it-toe)
The bill please A conta por favor (ah con-ta paw fav-aw)
Do you know where the bank is? Você sabe onde é o banco? (vos-say sa-beh onjy eh oh banko)
Do you have Wi-Fi? Você tem Wi-Fi? (vos-say tem Wi-Fi)
Where is the supermarket? Onde é o supermercado? (onjy eh oh super-mer-cah-do)
Do you have a room available? Você tem um quarto disponível? (vos-say tem um kwar-toe dis-pon-eve-el)
I want to go to the beach please, driver Quero ir a praia por favor conductor (ker-ro ih ah pri-ya paw favaw con-duck-tor)
What time is it? Que horas são? (kay hor-as so)
Can you call me a taxi? Pode me chamar um táxi? (po-gee meh cham-mar um taxi)
Is it walking distance? É uma curta distância? (eh uma kert-a dis-tance-iya)
My name is Mia Meu nome é Mia (meh-ooh nom-eh eh Mia)
What is your name? Qual é o seu nome? (kwal eh o se-ooh nom-eh)
I am from England Eu sou da Inglaterra (ee-yo sue da ing-la-ter-ha)
Where are you from? De onde você é? (je onjy vos-say eh)
Do you speak English? Fala inglês? (fal-la ing-lez)
How are you? Como você está? (com-mo vos-say es-ta)
I'm good Estou bom (es-too bom)
Can i use the bathroom? Posso usar o baneiro? (posh-o use-ar oh ban-yer-o)
